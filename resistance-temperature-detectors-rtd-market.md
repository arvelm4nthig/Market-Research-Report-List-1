<p><p>Resistance Temperature Detectors (RTDs) are temperature sensors that measure temperature by correlating the resistance of a material with temperature changes. The market for RTDs is segmented into three configurations: </p><p>1. **Two-wire Configuration**: Simplest design, most cost-effective but prone to errors due to lead resistance.  </p><p>2. **Three-wire Configuration**: Reduces errors by compensating for lead resistance, commonly used in industrial applications.  </p><p>3. **Four-wire Configuration**: Offers the highest accuracy by eliminating lead resistance altogether, ideal for precision measurements in laboratories and critical applications.</p></p>
