<p><p>The food-grade liquid nitrogen market primarily utilizes two methods for production: cryogenic distillation and pressure swing adsorption (PSA). Cryogenic distillation involves cooling air to extremely low temperatures to separate nitrogen from other gases effectively, ensuring high purity. In contrast, PSA uses varying pressures to capture nitrogen from air, making it a cost-effective method for producing nitrogen on-site. Both techniques cater to the growing demand for food preservation, flash freezing, and enhancing food quality, each offering distinct advantages in production efficiency and purity.</p></p>
