<p><ul><li>Anticoagulants</li><li>Fibrinolytics</li><li>Anti-Platelet Drugs</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Blood clot preventive drugs are categorized into three main types: anticoagulants, fibrinolytics, and anti-platelet drugs. Anticoagulants inhibit blood coagulation, reducing the risk of clots in conditions like atrial fibrillation. Fibrinolytics work to dissolve existing clots, used in emergencies like heart attacks. Anti-platelet drugs prevent platelet aggregation, decreasing clot formation risk. Each type serves distinct purposes in managing cardiovascular diseases and other conditions, contributing to improved patient outcomes and health management strategies in the market.</p></p>
