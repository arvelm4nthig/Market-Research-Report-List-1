<p><p>Pulmonary Arterial Hypertension (PAH) is a progressive disorder characterized by elevated blood pressure in the pulmonary arteries, leading to reduced blood flow and oxygen to the lungs. The market for PAH therapies includes various drug classes: Prostacyclin and its analogs, which promote vasodilation; soluble guanylate cyclase stimulators (SGC) that enhance nitric oxide signaling; endothelin receptor antagonists (ERA) targeting vasoconstrictor pathways; and phosphodiesterase-5 inhibitors (PDE-5) that improve blood flow by preventing the breakdown of cGMP. Each type plays a critical role in managing PAH symptoms and improving patient outcomes.</p></p>
