<p><p>The Long-Grain Rice Seed market comprises two primary types: Japonica and Indica rice. Japonica rice, characterized by its short, plump grains, is grown in cooler climates and is often sticky when cooked, making it popular in East Asian cuisine. In contrast, Indica rice features long, slender grains that remain separate when cooked, thriving in warmer regions and preferred in South Asian and Southeast Asian dishes. Both types fulfill diverse culinary needs and cater to various consumer preferences globally.</p></p>
