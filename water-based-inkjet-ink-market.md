<p><p>The water-based inkjet ink market features two primary types: pigment ink and dye ink. Pigment ink consists of solid particles suspended in liquid, offering superior lightfastness and durability, making it ideal for archival prints. In contrast, dye ink comprises colorant molecules dissolved in liquid, providing vibrant colors and smooth gradients but with lower resistance to fading and water. Both types cater to different printing needs, from professional photography to everyday documents, influencing choices based on desired quality and longevity.</p></p>
