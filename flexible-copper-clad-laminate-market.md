<p><p>The Flexible Copper-Clad Laminate market comprises various manufacturing techniques, each serving distinct applications. Tape casting involves spreading a slurry of copper onto a substrate, creating thin, flexible layers. Sputtering utilizes plasma to deposit a thin layer of copper, ensuring uniform coverage on flexible materials. Electroplating immerses substrates in a solution to deposit copper, enhancing conductivity. Laminating combines multiple layers of materials under heat and pressure, resulting in enhanced durability and flexibility. These methods cater to the growing demand in electronics and advanced materials.</p></p>
