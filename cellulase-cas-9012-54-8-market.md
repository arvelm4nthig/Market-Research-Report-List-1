<p><p>Cellulase (CAS 9012-54-8) is an enzyme that catalyzes the breakdown of cellulose into glucose, playing a significant role in various industries. The market for cellulase can be categorized into three main types: Endoglucanases (EG), which cleave internal bonds in cellulose; Cellobiohydrolases (CBH), which hydrolyze cellulose from the ends, releasing cellobiose; and β-glucosidases (BG), which convert cellobiose into glucose. Each type serves distinct functions, impacting applications in biofuels, textiles, food, and animal feed, enhancing efficiency and promoting sustainability.</p></p>
